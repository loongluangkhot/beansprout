# Product Brief: beansprout

<!-- Content will be appended sequentially through collaborative workflow steps -->

## Executive Summary

beansprout is a mobile-first, cross-platform app that facilitates social book clubs for casual readers seeking meaningful connections through reading and learning. Unlike traditional book apps that focus on tracking reads or discussing books academically, beansprout is a **connection app** where books are the vehicle and meaningful human connection is the destination.

The app addresses a fundamental human need: finding people who share our values and can meet emotional needs without requiring articulation — what we call **"emotional shorthand."** By combining accountability (to help users commit to reading) with emotional connection (to satisfy the deeper need for understanding), beansprout creates a unique value proposition that distinguishes it from existing solutions.

---

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

Casual readers face a unique challenge: they want the social benefits of book clubs — accountability, connection, meaningful discussions — but existing solutions fail to deliver. Current book club platforms focus on logistics or book discussions, but neglect the deeper emotional needs that drive participation.

The core problems are:
1. **Social anxiety barrier** — Shy participants feel excluded from discussions dominated by confident voices
2. **Lack of facilitation** — Without structure, meetups become unproductive or uncomfortable
3. **No accountability mechanism** — Readers struggle to commit to finishing books
4. **Superficial connections** — Existing apps facilitate scheduling but not meaningful relationships

### Problem Impact

Without a solution, casual readers:
- Feel isolated in their reading journey
- Struggle to find like-minded individuals for book clubs
- Experience anxiety about attending meetups
- Miss out on the dual benefit of accountability + emotional connection

This affects mental wellness — reading provides escape and growth, but without community, readers miss the amplified benefits of shared experience.

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

Current book club solutions focus on:
- **Logistics** — Scheduling, RSVPs, venue finding
- **Content** — Book recommendations, reading tracking
- **Discussion forums** — Async message boards

What's missing:
- **Facilitation tools** that make discussions accessible to all personality types
- **Structured formats** that lower anxiety and include shy participants
- **Accountability systems** that help users commit to reading
- **Connection-first approach** that prioritizes relationships over books

### Proposed Solution

beansprout is a connection-first platform where:

1. **Season-Based Model** — Book clubs run in 3-4 month "seasons" with themes, creating a gamified journey with lower commitment barriers

2. **Meetup Facilitation Tools (CORE)** — The defining feature:
   - 28+ discussion prompts across 6 categories (Icebreakers, Character, Theme, Personal Connection, Writing & Craft, Wrap-Up)
   - 6 structured meetup formats (Guided Discussion, Round-Robin, Theme Deep-Dive, Split Conversation, Chill Chat, ORID Deep-Dive)
   - Scrum retrospective adaptations (Mad/Sad/Glad, Start/Stop/Continue, 4Ls)

3. **User Profiles** — Bio, genres, reading history enabling compatibility matching

4. **RSVP + Attendance** — Tracking with gamification (points, badges)

5. **Organizer Controls** — Max members, absence thresholds to manage group health

### Key Differentiators

1. **Connection-First Philosophy** — Not a book app; a connection app where books are the vehicle
2. **Facilitation as Inclusion** — Structured meetups lower anxiety barrier for shy participants
3. **Dual Motivation Engine** — Accountability + emotional connection reinforce each other
4. **Season Model** — Creates achievement cycles while maintaining low commitment threshold
5. **Research-Backed Prompts** — Library developed from proven facilitation frameworks

## Target Users

### Primary User Segment: Young Working Adult Casual Readers

**Demographics:**
- Age: 22-35 (young professionals)
- Employment: Full-time working adults
- Reading habit: Casual/occasional readers (not hardcore bibliophiles)
- Tech comfort: High (mobile-first, cross-platform)
- Life stage: Early to mid-career, building social networks

**Core Characteristics:**
- Seeks meaningful social connections but values convenience
- Has limited time for socializing — wants quality over quantity
- Reads for escape, growth, and personal development
- Wants accountability to maintain reading habits
- Values authenticity and depth in relationships

**Goals & Motivations:**
1. **Connection** — Find like-minded people who share similar values
2. **Accountability** — Stay committed to reading through social commitment
3. **Growth** — Gain new perspectives through meaningful discussions
4. **Wellness** — Use reading as escape and stress relief
5. **Community** — Feel part of something larger than themselves

**Pain Points:**
- Social anxiety prevents attending meetups
- Feels intimidated by "serious" book clubs
- Struggules to find time for reading
- Finds existing book clubs too demanding or academic
- Wants deeper connections but finds small talk exhausting

### User Roles (All Members Have Equal Access)

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Season Member** | Joins and participates in a season/book club |
| **Season Creator** | Creates a new season with book selection and meetup schedule |
| **Meetup Host** | Hosts/organizes individual meetups (rotating or designated) |
| **Facilitator** | Any member can use facilitation tools — role assigned outside app |

**Key Design Principle:** All members have equal access to facilitation tools. The facilitator role is assigned outside the app, so any member can prepare and lead discussions using the app's tools.

---

### User Journey

**Discovery & Onboarding:**
1. User discovers beansprout through word-of-mouth, social media, or search
2. Downloads app (mobile-first) or visits web app
3. Creates profile: name, bio, favorite genres, reading history
4. Browses Season Library to find public seasons

**Joining a Season:**
1. Browses available seasons (by theme, location, schedule)
2. Views season details: book, duration, meetup schedule, member profiles
3. RSVPs to join season
4. Receives confirmation and season guidelines

**During Season (Ongoing):**
1. Receives reminders for upcoming meetups
2. RSVP to individual meetups
3. Attends meetups (in-person or virtual)
4. Uses facilitation tools (prompts, formats) to contribute to discussions
5. Earns points/badges for participation

**Success Moments:**
- First meaningful discussion where they share something personal
- Connecting with another member who shares similar values
- Completing a season and feeling a sense of achievement
- Attending a meetup they were nervous about and enjoying it

**Long-term Engagement:**
- Joins another season
- Creates their own season
- Recommends to friends
- Becomes regular facilitator using app tools

## Success Metrics

### User Success Metrics

The primary gauge of success is **season completion** — measuring whether users actually engage with the season rather than just signing up and lurking.

**Season Completion Definition:**
A user "completes" a season when they attend a minimum of **70% of scheduled meetups** in that season.

**Why 70%?**
- Allows for life contingencies (work travel, illness, emergencies)
- Ensures meaningful participation threshold
- Distinguishes active members from passive "lurkers"

**Supporting Success Indicators:**
- Users report **meaningful discussions** (qualitative feedback)
- Users feel **growth** (new perspectives gained)
- Users feel **wellness** (escape + connection benefits)
- Users make **meaningful connections** with other members

### Measurement Requirements

**Attendance System (Required for MVP):**
To accurately measure season completion and engagement, the app requires:
- **RSVP tracking** — Did user RSVP to meetup?
- **Attendance check-in** — Did user actually attend? (in-person or virtual)
- **No-show tracking** — User RSVP'd but didn't attend

This prevents users from signing up for seasons and lurking without participation being counted.

## MVP Scope

### Core Features (User Flow Order)

| # | Feature | Rationale |
|---|---------|-----------|
| 1 | **User Profiles** | Prerequisite — users need accounts first before any activity |
| 2 | **Season Library for Discovery** | Users need to find seasons to join |
| 3 | **Season Creation + Templates** | Users need ability to create seasons with book, duration, schedule |
| 4 | **RSVP + Attendance Tracking** | Essential for measuring success (70% threshold) |
| 5 | **Meetup Facilitation Tools (CORE)** | The key value proposition — 28+ prompts, 6 formats |
| 6 | **Basic Location (manual entry)** | Needed for meetup logistics (venue address) |
| 7 | **Basic Reminders** | Support engagement and reduce no-shows |

---

### MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is considered successful when:

| Criteria | Target |
|----------|--------|
| **Season Completion Rate** | >=50% of users who join a season complete it (attend >=70% of meetups) |
| **Attendance Rate** | >=70% of RSVPs result in actual attendance |
| **User Retention** | >=30% of users who complete a season join another season |
| **Facilitation Usage** | >=60% of meetups use facilitation tools (prompts/formats) |
| **User Feedback** | Users report meaningful discussions (qualitative validation) |

**Go/No-Go Decision Point:**
- If Season Completion Rate <30% after 3 months: Re-evaluate core value proposition
- If Attendance Rate <50%: Improve reminders and RSVP experience
