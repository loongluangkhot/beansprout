# Scrum Retrospective Tools for Book Clubs

## Overview

Scrum retrospectives are structured reflection sessions used by agile teams to improve their processes. Many of these frameworks translate surprisingly well to book club discussions, offering fresh ways to process reading experiences and improve the club experience.

---

## Framework 1: Mad, Sad, Glad ⭐

*Originally from agile retrospectives — adapted for emotional processing*

### Original Scrum Use
Team members share what made them mad, sad, or glad during the sprint.

### Book Club Adaptation

| Emotion | Book Club Prompt |
|---------|------------------|
| **Glad** | "What did you love about this book/character/moment?" |
| **Sad** | "What made you feel sad, disappointed, or emotional?" |
| **Mad** | "What frustrated you or made you angry?" |

### Example Book Club Exercise
> **"Chapter by Chapter: Mad, Sad, Glad"**
> 
> Go through the book together and have members share:
> - One moment that made them **glad** (happy, inspired, satisfied)
> - One moment that made them **sad** (moved, touched, melancholy)
> - One moment that made them **mad** (frustrated, annoyed, angry)
> 
> This works especially well for fiction with strong emotional arcs.

### Best For
- Emotional books (drama, tragedy, relationship stories)
- Processing intense reading experiences
- Getting different emotional reactions from the group

## Framework 2: Start, Stop, Continue

*Action-oriented reflection — very practical*

### Original Scrum Use
What should the team start doing, stop doing, and continue doing?

### Book Club Adaptation

| Category | Book Club Prompt |
|----------|------------------|
| **Start** | "What should we start doing in our book club?" |
| **Stop** | "What should we stop doing?" |
| **Continue** | "What should we keep doing?" |

### Example Book Club Exercise
> **"Our Season: Start, Stop, Continue"**
> 
> At the end of a season, discuss:
> - **Start:** Try a new genre? More discussion prompts? Virtual meetups?
> - **Stop:** Skip meetings when busy? Only discussing plot?
> - **Continue:** What made this season great?
> 
> Also works for the book itself:
> - Start reading more [genre]
> - Stop [character] from making that mistake
> - Continue exploring themes we love

### Best For
- Season wrap-up sessions
- Improving the book club experience
- Practical action planning

## Framework 3: 4Ls (Liked, Learned, Lacked, Longed For)

*Comprehensive reflection across positive and gaps*

### Original Scrum Use
What did we like? What did we learn? What did we lack? What did we long for?

### Book Club Adaptation

| L | Book Club Prompt |
|---|------------------|
| **Liked** | "What did you like about this book/meeting/season?" |
| **Learned** | "What did you learn (from the book or experience)?" |
| **Lacked** | "What was missing or felt lacking?" |
| **Longed For** | "What did you wish was different or more of?" |

### Example Book Club Exercise
> **"Season Reflection: The 4Ls"**
> 
> At end of a season, have each member fill in:
> - **Liked:** The discussions about friendship themes
> - **Learned:** About 1920s history I didn't know
> - **Lacked:** More time to discuss the ending
> - **Longed For:** More connection with newer members

### Best For
- Season retrospectives
- Evaluating what's working/not working in the club
- Holistic reflection

## Framework 4: ORID (Objective, Reflective, Interpretive, Decisional)

*Structured deep-dive from the Dialogue Café method*

### Original Scrum Use
Guided conversation from facts → feelings → meaning → action.

### Book Club Adaptation

| Stage | Question | Book Club Example |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| **Objective** | What happened? | "What were the main events in this section?" |
| **Reflective** | How did it make you feel? | "How did you feel when [event] happened?" |
| **Interpretive** | What does it mean? | "What do you think the author was trying to say?" |
| **Decisional** | What will we do? | "What will you do with what you learned?" |

### Example Book Club Exercise
> **"Deep Dive: ORID for a Chapter"**
> 
> For a pivotal chapter, guide discussion:
> 1. **O:** "What happened in this chapter?"
> 2. **R:** "How did you feel as you read it?"
> 3. **I:** "What do you think this means for the story/theme?"
> 4. **D:** "What will you take away from this?"

### Best For
- Literary fiction with subtext
- Nonfiction with actionable insights
- Groups that enjoy deeper analysis

## Framework 5: ROTI (Return on Time Invested)

*Evaluating worthwhileness of the experience*

### Original Scrum Use
Rate the time spent and identify improvements.

### Book Club Adaptation

> **"Was This Worth It?"**
> 
> At end of each meetup:
> - "On a scale of 1-10, was this meetup worth your time?"
> - "What made it worthwhile?"
> - "What would make it better next time?"
> 
> Also for the book:
> - "Was the time spent reading this book worth it?"
> - "Would you recommend the investment?"

### Best For
- Evaluating book choices
- Improving meetup quality
- Getting honest feedback

## Framework 6: Starfish (Keep, More, Less, Stop, Start)

*Fine-grained action planning*

### Original Scrum Use
Five actions: keep doing, do more of, do less of, stop doing, start doing.

### Book Club Adaptation

| Category | Book Club Prompt |
|----------|------------------|
| **Keep** | What should we definitely keep doing? |
| **More** | What should we do more of? |
| **Less** | What should we do less of? |
| **Stop** | What should we stop doing? |
| **Start** | What should we start doing? |

### Example Book Club Exercise
> **"Starfish for Our Club"**
> 
> - **Keep:** The themed discussions
> - **More:** Time for informal socializing
> - **Less:** Talking over each other
> - **Stop:** Ending meetings early without wrap-up
> - **Start:** Icebreaker questions each meetup

### Best For
- Season planning
- Action-oriented groups
- Continuous improvement

## Framework 7: Sailboat

*Visualizing what's helping and what's holding you back*

### Original Scrum Use
Team visualizes the sprint as a sailboat: winds (helps), anchors (hinders), rocks (risks), island (goal).

### Book Club Adaptation

> **"Our Reading Journey: Sailboat"**
> 
> Visualize your season as a boat journey:
> - **Wind:** What's pushing us forward? (Great discussions, supportive members)
> - **Anchor:** What's holding us back? (Busy schedules, no time to read)
> - **Rocks:** What could go wrong? (Missing members, disagreements)
> - **Island:** Where are we heading? (Finish the book, meaningful connections)
> - **Sun:** What's making this journey pleasant?

### Best For
- Visual learners
- Season kickoff or midpoint check-ins
- Identifying obstacles and supports

## Framework 8: ESVP (Explorer, Shopper, Vacationer, Prisoner)

*Understanding member engagement*

### Original Scrum Use
Team members identify their mindset: explorer (learning), shopper (finding value), vacationer (coasting), prisoner (stuck).

### Book Club Adaptation

> **"How Are You Showing Up?"**
> 
> Ask members to identify their current mindset:
> - **Explorer:** "I'm discovering new perspectives and ideas"
> - **Shopper:** "I'm here to find something valuable for myself"
> - **Vacationer:** "I'm just here to relax and enjoy"
> - **Prisoner:** "I feel obligated to be here"
> 
> Helps organizers understand group energy and adjust.

### Best For
- Understanding group dynamics
- Checking in on member satisfaction
- Adjusting meetup energy

## Recommendations for Your App

### MVP Integration
1. **Mad, Sad, Glad** — Simple, intuitive, works for any book
2. **Start, Stop, Continue** — Great for season wrap-ups
3. **4Ls** — Comprehensive reflection tool

### Future Enhancements
- ROTI ratings after each meetup
- Sailboat visualization (could be a fun UI element)
- ESVP check-in polls

*These frameworks can be added as "special formats" in your app, giving organizers additional structured options beyond the standard prompt-based discussions.*
