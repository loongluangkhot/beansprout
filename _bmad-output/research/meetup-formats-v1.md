# Meetup Format Templates

Choose the format that fits your group's energy, size, and time available. Each format includes timing suggestions, prompt recommendations, and facilitation tips.

---

## Format 1: Guided Discussion ⭐ (Most Popular)

*A structured meeting with pre-selected prompts flowing into conversation*

### Best For
- Groups of 4-8 members
- First-time organizers
- When you want to ensure all topics are covered

### Timing
| Segment | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Warm-up | 5 min | Arrivals, quick check-in |
| Icebreaker | 10 min | Low-pressure opening |
| Main Discussion | 30-40 min | 3-4 prompts, free-flow |
| Wrap-up | 10 min | Reflection, next book |
| **Total** | **55-65 min** | |

### Suggested Prompts
1. **Icebreaker:** "On a scale of 1-5, how would you rate this book?"
2. **Character:** "Which character did you relate to most?"
3. **Theme:** "What theme stayed with you?"
4. **Personal:** "Would you recommend this to a friend?"
5. **Wrap-up:** "If this book were a song, what would it be?"

### Facilitation Tips
- Share prompts in advance via the app
- Go around the circle to ensure everyone speaks
- If conversation stalls, move to next prompt
- Designate a time-keeper if needed

## Format 2: Round-Robin Check-In

*Each person shares one thought in sequence — low pressure, everyone participates*

### Best For
- New groups or shy members
- Groups that struggle with dominant voices
- Quick meetups (30-45 min)

### Timing
| Segment | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Quick Intro | 5 min | Who came, any announcements |
| Round-Robin | 20-30 min | Each person shares |
| Open Chat | 10 min | Follow up on interesting points |
| **Total** | **35-45 min** | |

### Suggested Prompts
- "What's one word for this book?"
- "What's one thing you took away?"
- "What's one question you still have?"
- "What's one scene that stuck with you?"

### Facilitation Tips
- Use a "pass" option — no one forced to share
- Go in alphabetical order or random
- Note interesting points for open chat
- Keep shares to 1-2 minutes each

## Format 3: Theme Deep-Dive

*Focus entirely on one theme or question — explore it from many angles*

### Best For
- Experienced groups wanting deeper discussion
- Books with strong thematic content
- When a particular topic resonates

### Timing
| Segment | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Theme Intro | 5 min | State the theme/topic |
| Individual Reflection | 5 min | Quiet think time |
| Discussion | 35-40 min | Exploring theme deeply |
| Personal Takeaway | 10 min | What does this mean for you? |
| **Total** | **55-60 min** | |

### Suggested Themes to Explore
- "What does the book say about [relationships/grief/growth]?"
- "How does the main character change, and what triggers it?"
- "What does the author want us to question?"
- "How does this book reflect the world we live in?"

### Facilitation Tips
- Choose theme based on book strength
- Give 5 min quiet time for thoughts to gather
- Ask "Why?" and "What makes you say that?"
- Bridge to personal connections

## Format 4: Split Conversation (Fishbowl Lite)

*Break into pairs, then reconvene — great for larger groups*

### Best For
- Groups of 8+ members
- When you want more talking time per person
- Mixing up energy

### Timing
| Segment | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Full Group Prompt | 5 min | Introduce the question |
| Pairs Discussion | 15-20 min | Deep dive in duos |
| Report Back | 15-20 min | Share highlights |
| Open Discussion | 10 min | Final thoughts |
| **Total** | **45-55 min** | |

### Suggested Prompts (pair-friendly)
- "What's one moment that surprised you?"
- "What would you ask the author if you could?"
- "Did anything in the book remind you of your life?"
- "What do you think happens next [for characters]?"

### Facilitation Tips
- Assign pairs randomly or let members choose
- Set clear time for pairs (use a timer)
- Ask each pair to note 1 thing to share
- Keep report-back brief (30 sec each)

## Format 5: Chill Chat (Casual)

*Informal, conversation-driven — prompts are optional backup*

### Best For
- Established groups comfortable with each other
- When members just want to hang out
- Post-holiday or busy periods

### Timing
| Segment | Duration | Purpose |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Mingling | 10 min | Arrivals, drinks, catching up |
| Optional Prompt | 10 min | If conversation needs a jumpstart |
| Open Chat | 25-30 min | Whatever the group wants to discuss |
| Wrap | 5 min | Next meetup确认 |
| **Total** | **45-55 min** | |

### Suggested Prompts (use as backup)
- "What's the best book you've read this year?"
- "What are you reading right now?"
- "Any book recommendations for the group?"
- "Any updates on your reading goals?"

### Facilitation Tips
- Don't force structure — trust the group
- Have 1-2 backup prompts ready
- Focus on community, not just the book
- Let conversations flow naturally

## Format 6: ORID Deep-Dive ⭐ (Scrum Adapted)

*Structured reflection from facts to feelings to meaning to action*

### Best For
- Literary fiction with subtext
- Nonfiction with actionable insights
- Groups that enjoy deeper analysis
- Pivotal chapters or scenes

### Origin
ORID comes from the **Dialogue Café** / **Agile Retrospective** framework: **O**bjective → **R**eflective → **I**nterpretive → **D**ecisional

### Timing
| Stage | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Objective | 10 min | What happened? (Facts) |
| Reflective | 10 min | How did it make you feel? (Reaction) |
| Interpretive | 20-25 min | What does it mean? (Understanding) |
| Decisional | 10-15 min | What will we do? (Action) |
| **Total** | **50-60 min** | |

### The 4 Stages in Detail

**Stage 1: Objective — What happened?**
Focus on facts from the book. No opinions yet.

- "What were the main events in this section?"
- "Who are the key characters and what did they do?"
- "What happened at the [pivotal scene]?"
- "What stood out in the writing or plot?"

**Stage 2: Reflective — How did it make you feel?**
Move to emotional response. This is where the book comes alive.

- "How did you feel as you read this?"
- "What surprised you?"
- "What moved you or stuck with you?"
- "Was there a moment that made you laugh, cry, or pause?"

**Stage 3: Interpretive — What does it mean?**
Now explore meaning, themes, and deeper understanding.

- "What do you think the author was trying to say?"
- "What does this tell us about [theme/character/human nature]?"
- "How does this connect to our own lives or experiences?"
- "What questions does this raise?"

**Stage 4: Decisional — What will we do?**
Bridge to action or personal commitment.

- "What will you take away from this?"
- "How will you apply what you learned?"
- "What's one thing you'll remember a month from now?"
- "What should we discuss next time?"

### Example: Applying ORID to a Chapter

> **Book:** "The Great Gatsby" — Chapter 5 (Gatsby & Daisy reunion)
> 
> **Objective:** "Gatsby asks Nick to arrange a meeting with Daisy. He cleans his house and buys flowers. When Daisy arrives, Gatsby is so nervous he knocks over a clock."
> 
> **Reflective:** "I felt nervous for Gatsby. It was almost painful to watch. I felt empathy for both him and Nick."
> 
> **Interpretive:** "This shows how idealized our memories can be. Gatsby isn't meeting Daisy — he's meeting his idea of Daisy. The clock breaking suggests time cannot be recreated."
> 
> **Decisional:** "I'm going to pay attention to how I idealize people in my own life. Also want to discuss whether Gatsby's optimism is beautiful or tragic."

### Facilitation Tips
- Move through stages in order — don't jump ahead
- Give equal time to each stage
- In Reflective stage, remind members: "No wrong feelings"
- In Decisional stage, keep actions personal (not group-wide)
- Works well for: specific chapters, key scenes, not entire books

## Quick Reference: Choosing a Format

| Factor | Recommended Format |
|--------|-------------------|
| New group | Guided Discussion |
| Shy members | Round-Robin |
| Large group (8+) | Split Conversation |
| Deep discussion wanted | Theme Deep-Dive, ORID Deep-Dive |
| Literary fiction / analysis | ORID Deep-Dive |
| Nonfiction with actions | ORID Deep-Dive |
| Established group | Chill Chat |
| Short on time (30 min) | Round-Robin |
| First meetup | Guided Discussion |
